Soil Erosion


Soil erosion affects food security, biodiversity, and climate stability. Over 33% of our planet's soil is already degraded, with an area the size of Portugal lost each year. Soil supports 95% of our food production and absorbs more carbon than forests yet it's rapidly disappearing.

Major areas that are impacted by soil erosion

Soil erosion depletes fertile topsoil, leading to reduced agricultural productivity, impacts water quality by increasing sedimentation in rivers and lakes, and damages infrastructure in affected areas. It also disrupts ecosystems by destroying habitats and contributing to desertification, especially in vulnerable regions like coastlines and mountain slopes.

Some major affected areas

Plateau, China

The Loess Plateau in China has faced extreme soil erosion, which degraded its productivity. Conservation projects have since revitalized the area, turning it into sustainable farmland.

Midwestern United States

The Midwestern United States experiences significant soil erosion due to intensive farming and rainfall runoff. Conservation practices like cover cropping and no-till farming aim to reduce erosion and improve soil health.

The Sahel, Africa

The Sahel region in Africa is severely affected by soil erosion, primarily due to drought, overgrazing, and deforestation, which degrade the soil and reduce its fertility. This erosion has led to desertification and threatens agriculture, impacting food security for millions.

Foothills, India and Nepal

The Himalayan Foothills in India and Nepal are prone to soil erosion from steep slopes and heavy monsoon rains. Reforestation and terracing efforts aim to mitigate this issue and preserve soil fertility.

African Highlands

The East African Highlands experience significant soil erosion from steep slopes and deforestation. Agroforestry and terracing are key strategies to help reduce erosion.

Australian Rangelands

The Australian Rangelands suffer soil erosion from overgrazing and drought. Sustainable grazing practices help reduce this impact.
